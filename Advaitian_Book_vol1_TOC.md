### Pillar 2 — The Twenty Universal Archetypes

*The catalogue of recurring structural patterns. Each chapter: vignette → archetype defined → worked examples → practice → master takeaways → bridge.*

| # | Chapter | Canonical takeaway |
|---|---------|--------------------|
| 1 | [Invariance](pillar2-archetypes/01-invariance.md) | Track the invariant; let everything else move. |
| 2 | [Symmetry](pillar2-archetypes/02-symmetry.md) | What is symmetric in the problem must be symmetric in the answer. |
| 3 | [Duality](pillar2-archetypes/03-duality.md) | Two viewpoints, primal and dual, describe the same structure with reversed roles. Choose the easier. |
| 4 | [Hidden Structure](pillar2-archetypes/04-hidden-structure.md) | Strip the surface notation; underneath is almost always a structure you already know. |
| 5 | [Substitution / Change of Variables](pillar2-archetypes/05-substitution.md) | The right change of variables turns a hard problem routine; the work is choosing it. |
| 6 | [Linearisation](pillar2-archetypes/06-linearization.md) | Replace the nonlinear object by its best linear approximation, then bound the error. |
| 7 | [Normalisation / Scaling](pillar2-archetypes/07-normalization.md) | Normalisation strips away every degree of freedom that doesn't matter; what remains is structure. |
| 8 | [Domain Translation](pillar2-archetypes/08-domain-translation.md) | The same structure looks different in each language; read it in the easiest one. |
| 9 | [Domain Constraints / Bounds](pillar2-archetypes/09-domain-constraints.md) | Algebra generates candidates; the domain selects the winner. |
| 10 | [Inequality Constraints](pillar2-archetypes/10-inequality-constraints.md) | Inequalities aren't obstacles but constraints; the equality case is the answer. |
| 11 | [Existence / Uniqueness Conditions](pillar2-archetypes/11-existence-uniqueness.md) | Prove it exists before you find it; existence and uniqueness are separate questions. |
| 12 | [Extremal Principles](pillar2-archetypes/12-extremal-principles.md) | The extremum is where structure becomes sharp — vertex, boundary, or interior critical point. |
| 13 | [Combinatorial Principles](pillar2-archetypes/13-combinatorial-principles.md) | Structure first, then count: find the symmetry or bijection before you enumerate. |
| 14 | [Parity / Modular Reasoning](pillar2-archetypes/14-parity-modularity.md) | Sometimes the remainder tells the whole story; the right modulus collapses the problem. |
| 15 | [Bijection / Correspondence](pillar2-archetypes/15-bijection-correspondence.md) | If two problems are isomorphic, solve the simpler one — the bijection is the proof. |
| 16 | [Reverse Engineering](pillar2-archetypes/16-reverse-engineering.md) | When the answer is given, the problem is to find the question. |
| 17 | [Degrees of Freedom Analysis](pillar2-archetypes/17-degrees-of-freedom.md) | Count parameters minus constraints first; the degrees of freedom scope the solution. |
| 18 | [Recursion / Induction](pillar2-archetypes/18-recursion-induction.md) | Solve one step, then propagate to infinity — find the right inductive parameter. |
| 19 | [Pivoting / Elimination](pillar2-archetypes/19-pivoting-elimination.md) | Simplify by subtraction: choose the pivot that removes the most, then iterate. |
| 20 | [Analogy / Transfer](pillar2-archetypes/20-analogy-transfer.md) | Solve it once and you've solved it everywhere — recognise the pattern, transfer the technique. |

*Sub-pillars: Structure Recognition (1–4) · Method Selection (5–8) · Constraint Exploitation (9–12) · Structural Reasoning / Counting (13–16) · Meta-Reasoning (17–20). Scaffold families recorded in Blueprint §10.2.2.*
